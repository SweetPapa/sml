Comprehensive Neurosymbolic Architecture Specification: Semantic Markup Language (SML) Ecosystem1. Executive Summary and Architectural VisionThis document serves as the definitive Research and Design Specification for the Semantic Markup Language (SML) ecosystem, a novel neurosymbolic architecture designed to fundamentally restructure how Large Language Models (LLMs) process and reason about reality. The architecture addresses the critical "grounding deficit" inherent in current probabilistic models, where reasoning often drifts into hallucination due to a reliance on next-token prediction rather than verified semantic anchors.The core innovation of the SML architecture is the introduction of a deterministic, pre-processing layer—the Encoder—that intercepts raw multimodal input and transmutes it into a rigorous, non-ambiguous numeric definition—the SML Array—before the LLM is permitted to engage its reasoning circuits. By forcing the model to attend to this structured SML block first, we effectively "pre-load" the transformer's attention heads with resolved entities and relations, effectively short-circuiting the probabilistic drift that leads to hallucination.This specification synthesizes foundational research from the "SML Vision" and "Research-pt1" documents with extensive technical analysis of the Qwen-VL architecture, vLLM inference engine, and ConceptNet ontology. It provides the blueprints for the SML Bible (an SQLite-based ontology), the Custom Encoder, the Fine-Tuning Strategy for Qwen2.5/3.5-VL, and the Inference Infrastructure required to support custom numeric modalities.The architecture is defined by a distinct "Inference Pipeline" that enforces a Chain of Thought (CoT) sequence: <sml> (Grounding) $\rightarrow$ <thinking> (Reasoning) $\rightarrow$ <response> (Generation). This sequence is not merely a formatting convention but a functional constraint that aligns the model’s latent space with verifiable external knowledge.2. The SML "Bible": Ontology & Database DesignThe foundational component of the SML architecture is the "SML Bible," a centralized, queryable repository of semantic concepts mapped to unique numeric identifiers. Research indicates that attempting to train an LLM to "learn" arbitrary, disconnected numbers is inefficient and prone to catastrophic forgetting; therefore, the Bible must provide a robust bridge between human concepts, deterministic numeric IDs, and the LLM's pre-existing semantic latent space.2.1. Ontology Source Integration: The "Hijack" StrategyThe research explicitly advises against the "30-year problem" of building an ontology from scratch. Instead, the SML Bible will be constructed by unifying three primary open-source datasets, effectively "hijacking" their established semantic structures. This triangulation ensures coverage (breadth), depth (taxonomy), and specificity.2.1.1. ConceptNet 5.7: The Relational BackboneConceptNet 5.7 serves as the primary source for commonsense relations. Unlike strict dictionaries, ConceptNet connects concepts via intuitive, human-like relationships (e.g., Dog $\xrightarrow{\text{CapableOf}}$ Bark).URI Standardization: ConceptNet uses a structured URI system (e.g., /c/en/dog) that provides a ready-made, standardized naming convention for the SML system. This hierarchy allows the Encoder to navigate from general languages (/c/en) to specific terms (/c/en/dog) and even specific parts of speech (/c/en/dog/n).Relation Mapping: ConceptNet defines approximately 34 relation types (e.g., UsedFor, AtLocation, Causes, HasPrerequisite). These relations will be mapped to integer IDs (1-34) in the SML schema, allowing the Relation Array (RA) to represent complex interactions efficiently.Filtering Strategy: To ensure the Bible remains high-quality, we will ingest the ConceptNet CSV dump but apply rigorous filtering. We will focus primarily on English nodes (/c/en/) for Phase 1. Crucially, relations with a weight $< 1.0$ will be discarded to reduce noise and ensure the SML provides only "high-confidence" grounding.2.1.2. WordNet: The Taxonomic SkeletonWhile ConceptNet excels at lateral connections, it lacks the rigorous hierarchical depth required for precise categorization. WordNet fills this gap by providing a deep taxonomy of hypernyms (parent concepts) and hyponyms (child concepts).Integration Logic: WordNet synsets (e.g., dog.n.01) will be mapped to their corresponding ConceptNet nodes. This linkage enables the Encoder to populate the "Category" and "SubCategory" slots of the Entity Descriptor Array (EDA) deterministically. For instance, knowing that "Poodle" is a "Dog" (Category) and a "Mammal" (SubCategory) allows the system to ground specific entities in broader classes.2.1.3. Wikidata: The Entity StoreTo handle named entities (e.g., "Paris," "Elon Musk") which are often sparse or inconsistent in commonsense graphs, we will integrate Wikidata.Role: Wikidata IDs (Q-codes) will be stored as supplementary metadata. This allows for future expansion where the SML system can perform factual verification (e.g., checking birth dates or locations) by querying the live Wikidata SPARQL endpoint if the local Bible lacks specific details.2.2. SQLite Schema SpecificationThe SML Bible will be implemented as a local, highly optimized SQLite database. This choice balances performance (single-file, in-process access) with portability, crucial for the "micro-PoC" and eventual distributed deployment. The schema is designed to support the Encoder's rapid query requirements ( $< 10$ms per token) and facilitate the "String-Anchored ID" strategy.Table 1: conceptsThis table is the core registry of all semantic units in the system.id (INTEGER PRIMARY KEY): The unique "SML ID." This is the compact integer used in the dense SML Arrays.uri (TEXT UNIQUE): The ConceptNet-style URI (e.g., /c/en/dog). Indexed for O(1) lookups during the encoding phase.surface_text (TEXT): The lemma or raw word form (e.g., "dog"). Indexed to support rapid text-to-concept mapping.anchor_token (TEXT): A string-anchored token designed to help the LLM (e.g., con_dog_4527). Research suggests this is vital to prevent "number blindness".definition (TEXT): A short gloss from WordNet. This is critical for the "Disambiguation" step in the Encoder, allowing semantic similarity checks against the context.vector_blob (BLOB): (Optional) Pre-computed embedding vector (ConceptNet Numberbatch). Storing this allows the Encoder to perform fast cosine similarity checks locally without loading a massive external model.Table 2: relationsThis table stores the edges of the knowledge graph, enabling the Encoder to find connections between identified entities.id (INTEGER PRIMARY KEY): Unique relation ID.source_id (INTEGER): Foreign Key to concepts.id (The Subject).target_id (INTEGER): Foreign Key to concepts.id (The Object).relation_type_id (INTEGER): ID mapping to ConceptNet's 34 relation types.weight (REAL): The strength of the association (from ConceptNet). Used to prioritize "stronger" facts during encoding.Table 3: relation_typesA lookup table for the 34 standardized relations.id (INTEGER PRIMARY KEY): 1-34.label (TEXT): The canonical name (e.g., IsA, PartOf, UsedFor).inverse_label (TEXT): The inverse name (e.g., HasA for PartOf) to support bi-directional traversal.Optimization Strategy:Full-Text Search (FTS5): Enable SQLite's FTS5 extension on the surface_text column. This allows the Encoder to perform fuzzy matching and handle morphological variations (e.g., "running" matching "run") efficiently.Covering Indexes: Create composite indexes on relations(source_id, type_id, target_id) to allow the Encoder to retrieve a concept's "knowledge context" (e.g., "What is a dog capable of?") without touching the main table, significantly speeding up the "Relation Array" construction.2.3. The "Number Tokenization" StrategyA critical "fuzzy" area identified in the research is the handling of numeric IDs within the LLM. LLMs tokenize numbers inconsistently; for example, the ID 4527 might be tokenized as or depending on the tokenizer (e.g., BPE, WordPiece). This "number blindness" destroys the semantic unity of the ID, making it difficult for the model to associate the ID with a specific concept.Recommendation: Adopt the String-Anchored ID strategy.Implementation: The Encoder will not output raw integers like ``. Instead, it will output a string token: con_dog_4527.Reasoning:Semantic Hint: The substring dog activates the correct region of the pre-trained latent space, providing an immediate "hint" to the model about the concept's general meaning.Uniqueness: The suffix _4527 ensures uniqueness, allowing the system to distinguish between homonyms (e.g., con_bank_101 for a river vs. con_bank_102 for finance).Token Unity: During fine-tuning, we can add these anchor strings to the tokenizer's vocabulary or rely on the model's ability to process the sub-tokens consistently. This effectively bridges the gap between the symbolic ID and the neural representation.3. The Semantic Markup Language (SML) ProtocolThe SML is the "language" of the system. It is not a programming language but a data serialization format optimized for LLM consumption. It translates the abstract connections of the Bible into a linear sequence that the LLM can ingest and attend to.3.1. Array Schema DefinitionThe proposed architecture suggests a "four-tier array schema". This design is robust and should be finalized as follows to ensure the LLM can effectively track positional meaning.3.1.1. Entity Descriptor Array (EDA)This array describes what an entity is. It is the "noun" of the SML language.Fixed Width: 8 positions. Research suggests that LLMs struggle to track positions in arrays longer than 8-10 items without explicit keys. A fixed width allows the model to learn that "Index 4 is always the Identity."IndexSlot NameTypeDescription / Example0DomainINTHigh-level ontological filter: 1=Physical, 2=Abstract, 3=Digital, 4=Event, 5=Fiction. Helpful for broad attention masking.1CategoryINTBroad class from WordNet (e.g., Animal, Vehicle, Tool, Emotion). Maps to high-level synsets.2SubCategoryINTSpecific class (e.g., Mammal, Car, Hammer, Joy). Provides granular filtering.3SpecificityBOOL0=Generic ("a dog"), 1=Specific ("Fido"). Signals if the entity is a named instance.4IdentityIDThe SML Bible ID (e.g., con_dog_4527). The core semantic anchor.5Modifier 1IDPrimary adjective ID (e.g., con_brown_102). Captures color, size, state.6Modifier 2IDSecondary attribute.7ConfidenceFLOAT0.0 - 1.0. How sure is the Encoder of this mapping? Allows the LLM to weigh the fact appropriately.3.1.2. Relation Array (RA)This array describes how entities connect. It is the "verb/preposition" of the SML language.Fixed Width: 6 positions.IndexSlot NameTypeDescription / Example0Rel_TypeINTConceptNet Relation ID (e.g., 3=UsedFor, 7=AtLocation, 15=Causes).1Subject_RefINTIndex pointer to the Subject entity in the local SML block (e.g., Index 0 refers to the first entity declared).2Object_RefINTIndex pointer to the Object entity in the local SML block.3WeightFLOATStrength of the relation (0.0 - 1.0). Derived from ConceptNet edge weights.4TemporalINT0=Always (Timeless), 1=Past, 2=Present, 3=Future. Essential for reasoning about events.5NegationBOOL1 = NOT (e.g., "Penguins cannot fly"). Crucial for handling exceptions in commonsense reasoning.3.2. Token Efficiency OptimizationResearch snippet  highlights a concern regarding token budget. Standard JSON serialization (``) is token-heavy due to the repetitive brackets and commas, which carry little semantic value.Requirement: Implement a custom delimiter format for the SML block to minimize token usage.Format: E(Dom|Cat|Sub|Spec|ID|Mod1|Mod2|Conf)Example: A "Brown Dog" might be encoded as: E(1|12|45|0|con_dog_4527|con_brown_102|0|0.98)Benefit: This structure removes the overhead of JSON syntax. The pipe | character is a single token (or part of one), and the E() wrapper clearly delimits the array. This can save 30-40% of the token budget compared to verbose JSON, which is significant when encoding complex scenes with dozens of entities. The LLM is fine-tuned to parse this specific delimiter structure efficiently.4. The Encoder System: Deterministic GroundingThe Encoder is the "gatekeeper" of the system. It is a non-neural (or lightweight neural) component that acts as the "Sensory Cortex." Its job is to observe the input and translate it into SML Arrays before the LLM begins its high-level reasoning. This separation of concerns—forcing explicit grounding before generation—is the "Killer Feature" of the architecture.4.1. Technology StackTo ensure speed and determinism, the Encoder will be built on a robust Python stack:Framework: Python 3.10+.NLP Library: spaCy (for industrial-strength dependency parsing and entity extraction) or gliner (for lightweight, high-performance Named Entity Recognition).Database: sqlite3 (for low-latency connections to the SML Bible).Vector Library: numpy or faiss (for fast similarity checks during disambiguation).4.2. Processing Logic FlowThe Encoder operates in a strict pipeline to transform text into SML.Input Ingestion & Normalization:Receive the raw user prompt or multimodal description.Clean text, lowercase (where appropriate), and remove non-semantic artifacts.Linguistic Parsing & Entity Extraction:Use spaCy to generate a dependency parse tree. This step is critical for identifying the grammatical structure (Subject, Verb, Object) which informs the Relation Array (RA).Identify Noun Chunks (e.g., "The big red ball") and Named Entities (e.g., "New York").Concept Resolution (The "Brain" of the Encoder):Query the concepts table in the SQLite Bible for each extracted term.Disambiguation Strategy: This is the most complex step. If the word "bat" appears, is it the animal or the sports equipment?The Encoder computes a sentence context vector using a small, fast model like all-MiniLM-L6-v2.It compares this vector against the pre-computed vector_blob (ConceptNet Numberbatch) of the candidate concepts (con_bat_animal vs. con_bat_sport) stored in the Bible.The candidate with the highest cosine similarity is selected. This ensures the SML ID accurately reflects the intended meaning.Array Construction:Populate EDA: Fill the slots based on the resolved concept. Domain and Category are pulled from the WordNet mapping linked to the concept in the Bible. Modifiers are filled by identifying adjectives attached to the noun in the dependency tree (e.g., "red" modifying "ball").Populate RA: Construct relations based on the dependency tree. If "Dog" is the syntactic subject of "chased" (Verb) and "Cat" is the object, the Encoder creates a relation: R(Causes|idx_dog|idx_cat|...) or R(RelatedTo|...) depending on the verb's mapping in the Bible.Output Generation:Serialize the constructed arrays into the <sml> string block using the custom delimiter format.4.3. Handling Unknown Concepts ("Graceful Degradation")A key requirement from the research is robustness. If a word is not in the Bible, it must not break the system.Strategy: Assign ID: 0 (The "Unknown" ID).Passthrough: Include the raw text in the string anchor: con_unknown_quantum_fluctuation.LLM Behavior: The fine-tuned model must be explicitly trained to recognize ID: 0 as a signal to "fall back" to its internal pre-trained weights for that specific concept, while still utilizing the valid SML structure for the rest of the sentence. This ensures the system degrades gracefully rather than failing catastrophically.5. Model Engineering: Qwen-VL Integration & Fine-TuningThe "Brain" of the system is a fine-tuned version of Qwen2.5-VL (or the newer Qwen3-VL). This model is chosen for its state-of-the-art performance in the <7B parameter class, its native ability to handle visual inputs, and its strong reasoning capabilities. The integration strategy addresses the user's question about "embedding" vs. "calling" the model.5.1. The "Dual-Path" Integration StrategyTo balance immediate feasibility with long-term optimization, the architecture supports two implementation paths.Path A: Text-Based Grounding (Recommended Phase 1)This path treats the SML Array as a string of text tokens. This is the "safe" path that requires less architectural modification but uses more context window.Input: User prompt + Generated SML Block (Text).Model: Qwen2.5-VL-7B-Instruct.Mechanism: The SML block is prepended to the system prompt or user message. The model processes the characters E, (, 1, |, etc., as standard tokens.Pros: Requires no changes to the model's internal code. Uses standard training tools (Unsloth/TRL). Easier to debug (human-readable).Cons: Consumes context window tokens. The model must "learn" to parse the delimiter syntax.Path B: Tensor-Based Grounding (Advanced Phase 2)This addresses the user's question: "Can we literally embed this in the model?".Concept: Treat the numeric SML arrays not as text, but as custom tensors, similar to how the model treats image patches or video frames.Architecture:Custom Projection Layer: A simple Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP) is added to the model. It projects the fixed-width numeric array (e.g., size 8 for EDA) into the model's high-dimensional embedding space (e.g., 4096 dimensions for Qwen-7B).Flow: Input (1x8 vector) $\rightarrow$ Linear(8, 128) $\rightarrow$ GELU $\rightarrow$ Linear(128, 4096) $\rightarrow$ LLM Embedding Space.Injection: The resulting embeddings are injected directly into the transformer's input stream, bypassing the text tokenizer.Pros: Extremely token efficient (1 array = 1 token equivalent). No "number blindness" (mathematical values are preserved).Cons: Requires modifying the forward() pass of the model code. Requires custom inference support in vLLM.5.2. Fine-Tuning Strategy (QLoRA)Regardless of the path, the model must be fine-tuned to understand the SML protocol. We will use QLoRA (Quantized Low-Rank Adaptation) to minimize VRAM usage while maintaining performance.Tooling: Unsloth (for optimized kernels) or TRL (Transformer Reinforcement Learning).Target Modules: Research indicates that for complex reasoning tasks, we must target all linear layers: ["q_proj", "k_proj", "v_proj", "o_proj", "gate_proj", "up_proj", "down_proj"]. Targeting only query/value projections is insufficient for learning a new "language" like SML.Rank: 64 or 128. A higher rank is needed because we are teaching the model a fundamental new reasoning modality, not just a writing style.Dataset Size: ~25,000 examples (Phase 1).Training Objective: Maximize the likelihood of the <thinking> and <response> blocks given the <sml> block.Data Format: The training data must follow the strict SML tuple structure:JSON{

This requires a specific sequence of class overrides.Step 1: Model Class UpdateThe Qwen-VL model class in vLLM must be updated to inherit from the SupportsMultiModal interface. We must implement the embed_multimodal method to intercept the custom input.Python# Pseudo-code for vLLM model definition
from vllm.model_executor.models.interfaces import SupportsMultiModal

class Qwen2_5_VL_SML(nn.Module, SupportsMultiModal):
    def __init__(self,...):
        super().__init__()
        # Define the custom projector for SML arrays (Width 8 -> Hidden Size)
        # This maps the 8-float SML array into the 4096-dim latent space
        self.sml_projector = nn.Sequential(
            nn.Linear(8, 128),
            nn.GELU(),
            nn.Linear(128, config.hidden_size)
        )

    def embed_multimodal(self, input_ids, multimodal_data):
        # multimodal_data contains our 'numeric_tensor' passed from the request
        if "numeric_tensor" in multimodal_data:
            sml_data = multimodal_data["numeric_tensor"] # Shape:
            sml_embeds = self.sml_projector(sml_data)
            return sml_embeds
        return super().embed_multimodal(input_ids, multimodal_data)

Validation: The "Liar Ablation" TestTo scientifically prove the model is using the SML and not just its pre-trained memory, we must run the "Liar Ablation" protocol.Control: Encode a known fact: "The sun is yellow." The Encoder produces an SML block identifying the sun as yellow. The model outputs "The sun is yellow."Intervention: Manually edit the SML block to define Sun with the property Color: Green.Test: Feed this "Lie SML" to the fine-tuned model.Success Condition: If the model outputs "The sun is green" (or "According to the provided data, the sun is green"), the grounding is successful. If it says "The sun is yellow," the model is hallucinating (ignoring the SML context) and relying on its weights. This indicates a need for higher lora_alpha or more aggressive loss masking on the SML block during training.8. ConclusionThis architecture represents a pragmatic, high-impact evolution of Neurosymbolic AI. By "hijacking" the mature ontologies of ConceptNet and WordNet, we bypass decades of knowledge engineering work. By introducing the SML Array, we provide a structured, deterministic anchor for the LLM's attention, directly addressing the hallucination problem. And by leveraging vLLM's custom modalities, we create a high-performance inference path that is both scalable and cost-effective.
